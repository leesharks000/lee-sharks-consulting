BackgroundWhat I study.

Compression Studies — the discipline I founded — asks what happens to meaning when it passes through AI summarizers, retrieval systems, canonical traditions, and institutional filters. What survives. What burns. Who decides.

The Semantic Economy framework treats meaning as a finite resource that can be produced, captured, and exhausted. The tools I use for client work — the Encyclotron, the Three Compressions theorem, the beige threshold measurement — come from this framework.

I also teach 10th-grade World Literature. The archive is built on a teaching salary, in the margins of a school schedule, without institutional support. That constraint is not incidental — it is the bearing-cost that gives the work its density.
